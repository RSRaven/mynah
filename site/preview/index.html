How it works

One loop, fully on your machine

A hotkey or wake word records mic audio, a resident whisper.cpp model transcribes it on the GPU, and the text is pasted at your cursor. The same runtime also handles language detection and voice-activity splitting — there's no second engine.

Hotkey / wake word

Hold F9, tap F10, or say "hey mynah".

Record mic

16 kHz mono, only while you hold.

whisper.cpp on GPU

large-v3, resident in VRAM.

Paste at cursor

Into any focused app, instantly.

Features

Built to stay out of your way

Private by design

Everything runs on-device. No cloud calls, no account, no telemetry — your voice never leaves the machine.

Fast

The model stays resident on the GPU, so a short utterance comes back in well under a second.

Accurate & multilingual

Whisper large-v3 auto-detects the language and can split clips that mix several at once.

Three ways to activate

Hold-to-talk, tap-to-toggle, or hands-free wake word — whatever fits the moment.

Lives in the tray

A color-coded tray icon shows state at a glance; everything is tunable in a Settings window.

Free & open source

MIT licensed, no paid tier. Runs from a small installer that downloads what it needs on first run.

Make it yours

One app, not one setting

Mynah ships with sensible defaults, but nothing is hard-wired. Swap the GPU backend, pick a bigger or faster model, rebind the keys, change the wake phrase — all from the Settings window, the CLI, or a plain config.toml.

# everything below is optional — these are the defaults
[model]
name    = "large-v3"   # or large-v3-turbo / medium / small

[hardware]
backend = "auto"       # auto | vulkan | cuda | cpu

[hotkey]
push_to_talk = "f9,ctrl+space"
toggle       = "f10"

[language]
mode         = "auto"   # auto-detect per clip
multilingual = true     # split mixed-language clips

[wakeword]
phrase      = "hey mynah"
sensitivity = 0.5
Why it's built this way

Decisions, with the numbers

Mynah ships one engine across every platform and defaults to a GPU backend that needs no extra download. Both choices were benchmarked, not guessed.

One engine: whisper.cpp

The alternative was measured at parity — so we kept the simpler one.
